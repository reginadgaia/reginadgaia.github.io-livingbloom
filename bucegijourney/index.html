The Focus You cannot think your way out of chronic indecision. We enter the mountain with intention, bypassing the analytical mind and using physical movement to rebuild your capacity to feel and trust your inner power.

The Real-Life Shift You cure the "Maybe Next Year" habit. You leave feeling capable and grounded, equipped with an actionable blueprint: knowing exactly where to draw firm boundaries and what specific path to confidently step onto next.

Why the Bucegi?
Why this moment?

Some places do more than offer a beautiful backdrop; their sheer physical scale puts our daily worries back into proportion. We chose the Bucegi Mountains because this landscape acts as a natural reset for your nervous system. Far from the cramped, relentless noise of urban life, the vastness of the high plateau, the deep valleys, the lake, the cave, and the crisp silence surrounding the ancient Sphinx rock formation create a physical environment where your mind naturally quiets.

These landscapes provide the ideal conditions for meeting yourself, gaining perspective, and true reconnection.

The Ideal Timing

September is the ideal moment. It is the closing of summer—a time to gather strength and insight before the season turns and the year rushes toward its end. This retreat is designed for that exact timing: when you don’t need more stimulation, but a meaningful pause. The summer crowds have left the Bucegi plateau, leaving quiet trails, crisp air, and the perfect conditions to fully immerse yourself in the journey.

How the journey works

The 5-Day Strategic Reset

"We do not force breakthroughs; we create the right conditions for them."

This journey follows a carefully guided progression through the phases of the Embodied Alignment Process: release, reconnection, and direction. Each day weaves together embodiment practices, nature experiences, and guided meditations, ensuring your insights do not remain abstract—they become a grounded, lived experience.

The experience is moderate and energizing rather than extreme. We use the cave, the forest, the mountain to awaken the body and sharpen perception, keeping the process grounded, structured, and deep.

1
Day 1 • Unplug & Drop In

Grounding and Intention

Your journey begins in the mountain town of Bușteni. The moment you arrive, the requirement to "perform" ends. This first evening is dedicated to actively unplugging—stepping out of your daily responsibilities and allowing your nervous system to recognize it is safe to rest.

At 17:00, we gather for our Opening Circle—the true start of the retreat. Here, we leave our external roles at the door, set our intentions for the days ahead, and connect with the group. We end the day with a warm, shared dinner at the hotel.

Note: Check-in at Hotel Steaua Nordului is available from 14:00.

2
2
Day 2 • Unplug & Drop In

The Descent — Letting Go

This morning begins with an embodiment practice designed to gently pull you out of your head and into your physical senses. After breakfast, we drive to the Ialomiței Cave. Descending into the quiet depth of the cave offers a visceral experience of "dropping in."

Following a shared lunch, we take a peaceful walk by Lake Bolboci. In the afternoon, we gather for a guided drum meditation in the woods—using rhythm as a grounded tool to physically discharge stored tension.

Note: We recommend bringing an extra empty bottle to collect fresh mountain water from the cave.

3
3
Day 3 • The Ascent

The Ascent — Gaining Perspective

Following an early breakfast, we head up to the high Bucegi plateau for a full day of active pilgrimage toward Mount Omu. This hike is the physical core of our Recalibration phase. We use the steady, rhythmic movement of walking to bypass the over-analytical mind.

After a well-earned picnic lunch on the mountain, enjoying the vast views, we hike to the Caraiman mountain hut. Weather permitting, we trade the hotel for an authentic high-altitude experience. We will share a sunset practice, a hearty warm dinner, and sleep close to the stars.

Note: Accommodation at the hut is in shared, rustic dorms. No showers available for this night.

4
4
Day 4 • Recalibration

The Plateau — Finding Clarity

We wake up at the mountain hut, starting our day with a grounding embodiment practice to anchor the strength built from yesterday's hike. Today is the culmination of our inner work, spent near the legendary Sphinx and Babele stone formations.

By now, the question shifts from "What is wrong?" to "What is now clear?" Near the ancient stones, we will hold a guided drum journey to access a renewed sense of personal power and a clear direction.

We will enjoy a picnic on the plateau before beginning our descent back to Bușteni. You will have free time to rest before we gather for a night integration circle, followed by dinner.

5
5
Day 5 • Integration

Integration and Return

The journey doesn't end on the mountain; it ends when we return home. On this final morning, we seal the process. After a gentle morning practice and breakfast, we will gather for our closing circle.

Through vision-based exercises, final integration work, and personal guidance, we will connect your restored clarity and courage directly to your renewed direction. The goal is for you to leave feeling capable and grounded.

We share one last lunch together. Departure is at 14:00.

Note on the itinerary: The mountain always sets the final conditions. While we maintain a clear structure and intention for the journey, the exact daily flow and activities may be adjusted depending on weather or group safety. The core arc of the retreat remains intact no matter the conditions.

Questions You Are Asking Yourself

Transparency. No fine print.

The Experience

Do I need to be very spiritual to join?

No. This retreat is designed for people who want something deeper, but may not identify strongly with spiritual labels. The experience includes reflection, ritual, and symbolic places, yet it is held in a grounded and accessible way.

Is it going to be too ‘weird’ or esoteric for me?

We are grounded. Yes, we use drums and rituals, but we are not floating in the clouds. You don't need to "believe" in anything—you just need to be open to the experience. And have strong intention!

Are psychedelics used?

We do not work with any substance nor plant medicine.

I am an introvert / I need my space.

Except in the mountain hut, where we all share the same space, you will have your own space (or shared with one person). We honor silence as much as we honor connection. You are never forced to share more than you want.

I booked a private room. Why do I have to share at the mountain hut and are there showers?

The night at Caraiman Hut (2,025m) is an authentic mountain experience. Due to the remote location and limited resources of high-altitude refuges, all lodging is in shared dorms and there are no shower facilities for this specific night. This one night of shared "mountain living" is a key part of the group journey and the Ascent phase. Your private room and a hot shower will be waiting for you back in Bușteni the following day.

Are the hikes very intense?

No. You need to be healthy and active. The retreat is moderate and energising rather than extreme. You should be comfortable walking on mountain terrain for several hours, but this is not designed as a high-performance trekking experience. We are hiking on the mountains (Bucegi Plateau) and walking in nature daily. On one of the days we may hike between 6 and 8 hours at a moderate pace. We will shorten the distance in case of bad weather conditions. We will not hike in steep areas, but on the plateau. The purpose of the hiking is perspective, embodiment, and connection — not physical proving.

Will I have internet and phone signal during the retreat?

In the town of Bușteni and at our hotel, connectivity is reliable. On the mountain plateau, signal exists but can be intermittent or weak depending on the specific location. Regarding the night at the Caraiman Hut, we are currently verifying the specific Wi-Fi and signal status and will update this information soon. However, we encourage you to view the mountain phase as a valuable opportunity to "unplug" and fully immerse yourself in the environment.

What kind of gear do I need for the hiking?

Good hiking boots are essential for the plateau terrain. You will also need layers of clothing to adjust to changing mountain temperatures, and a reliable rain-proof jacket or rain poncho is mandatory. Walking poles are useful for additional stability on the trail, but they are not compulsory. Don't worry about the specifics yet—a complete, detailed list of what to bring will be sent to you after registration.

What happens if the weather changes?

The mountain always sets the final conditions. We keep a clear structure and intention for the journey, while remaining flexible with exact logistics if weather or safety requires adjustment. The inner arc of the retreat remains intact even if practical details shift.

Is this retreat suitable if I am in a life or career transition?

Yes. In fact, that is one of the clearest fits for this retreat. It is designed for moments when something is shifting, when perspective is needed, and when the next chapter has not yet fully clarified itself.

Logistics & Food

Why is food not included in the price?

Freedom. We do not provide meals directly. Instead, you choose from local menus at restaurants and taverns to ensure you eat exactly what suits your personal needs. For picnics, we visit a supermarket together so you can select your own supplies. (Estimated budget approx. €30-40/day).

How do you handle allergies or dietary limitations?

Since you choose your own restaurant meals and supermarket supplies, you have full control over your diet during most of the retreat. However, for the dinner and breakfast at Caraiman Hut, please inform us of any severe allergies or dietary limitations during registration. We will coordinate with the hut managers to see what is possible. For those with highly restrictive diets, we strongly recommend purchasing additional safe snacks or supplies during our supermarket trip in Bușteni as a reliable backup.

How do I get to Busteni from Bucharest?

1. The Easy Way: Book our optional Group Transfer (€90 round trip). We pick you up in Bucharest airport and drop you at the hotel.

2. The Independent Way:

  • By Group Transfer: You can book a direct bus transfer independently from the airport to Bușteni. Check options and request hotel drop-off at Autogari.ro.
  • By Train: From Otopeni Airport (station Aeroport Henri Coandă), take the direct airport train to București Nord. From there, transfer to a train bound for Bușteni. You can search for the full route and purchase tickets via the CFR Calatori website.
Can I come alone?

Yes. Coming alone is completely welcome, and many people find that solo arrival allows them to enter the retreat more openly. The group setting is intentionally warm, intimate, and supportive. If you choose the Shared Room option, we will match you with another participant of the same gender.
